Welcome to the Cabin

The Knaphill Cabin is free, and open to anyone aged 11-17. You are welcome to drop by any time - all we ask is that you sign up to our Code of Conduct. This basically boils down to respect: when you come to the Knaphill Cabin you can expect to be treated with respect, and in return you will be expected to show respect to others: young people, volunteers, the building and equipment. Our behavioural policy is there for everyone's enjoyment - members who stick to it are eligible for our free positive behaviour trips.

Knaphill Cabin Code of Conduct

The type of behaviour that is promoted, praised and considered positive & acceptable at the Cabin include:

  • Being helpful, organising activities, clearing away, tidying litter etc.
  • Respect for others at the centre, peers, visitors and volunteers.
  • Respect for the property, resources and equipment that is used.
  • Being a role model to others at the centre, giving encouragement and assistance when and if needed.
The following are the examples of behaviour that would be considered negative & unacceptable at the Cabin:
  • Use of intimidating, threatening or bullying behaviour or language.
  • Abusive, racist, homophobic, sexist or other discriminatory or insulting language.
  • Possession / taking of drugs, including alcohol, or any illegal substances.*
  • Fighting and violent behaviour, including "play-fighting".
  • Disrespectful behaviour towards anyone at the Cabin.
  • Deliberate damage to property and vandalism.
  • Possession / use of weapons.
* Any person entering The Cabin who is thought to have taken any illegal substances or alcohol will be asked to leave immediately. In line with UK law, smoking is not allowed anywhere inside the Vyne.

Excursions

The Knaphill Cabin organises free trips for regular members, focussed on encouraging positive behaviour at sessions. Our current programme is kindly funded by Woking Borough Council's Community Support Scheme and Small Grants Scheme (Youth). Previously we have been donutting, go-karting and Go-Ape and paintballing.

All young people are welcome to discuss ideas for the Cabin with volunteers.

Safe Guarding

If you are a young person with difficulties or issues you would like to discuss, please talk to one of our volunteers and ask for help. The Knaphill Cabin Safeguarding Officers can also be contacted via: help@knaphillcabin.org.uk. We will reply to every message, but please be aware this may take a few days.

Help for young people is also available from the following organisations:
  • Child Line Call 0800 1111 for free or visit www.childline.org.uk.
  • Kidscape Call 0207 730 3300 or visit www.kidscape.org.uk.
  • NCH the children's charity Call 0117 935 4440 or visit www.nch.org.uk.
  • NSPCC Call 0808800500, email help@nspcc.org.uk or visit www.nspcc.org.uk.
  • Write or email Within Surrey you can contact Social Services on 0300 200 1006 and Surrey Police on 101 (in an emergency dial 999).
